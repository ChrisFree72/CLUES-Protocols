Overview

Two lipid extraction methods are being evaluated side-by-side for 13mm glass fiber filter punches loaded with 1mL blood or placenta digest. Both use chilled solvents, ice bath sonication (30 min), and 4°C centrifugation throughout. Sonication is performed in 20mL glass tubes (16×150mm) with the punch present. Extracts are then transferred to 12mL glass tubes (16×100mm) for phase separation, leaving the punch behind. 1mL of the target phase is collected, dried under nitrogen, and reconstituted in a fixed volume of 1:1 chloroform:methanol.

Extraction Protocols

ℹ️
All solvents chilled. Work on ice throughout. Centrifuge at 4°C. Lipid-rich phase collects at top.

Tube 1 — extraction with punch (20mL, 16×150mm)

  1. 1Place 13mm glass fiber punch into a 20mL glass test tube (16×150mm).
  2. 2Add 1.5mL cold methanol. Pre-soak punch for 5–10 minutes on ice to wet the glass fiber matrix and begin lipid release.
  3. 3Add 2.5mL cold MTBE. Vortex briefly to mix.
  4. 4Sonicate for 30 minutes in an ice bath. Monitor ice bath temperature — replenish ice if needed to maintain near 0°C.
  5. 5Transfer extract to a 12mL glass tube (Tube 2, 16×100mm), leaving punch behind.
  6. 6Rinse Tube 1 and punch with 0.25mL cold MTBE. Add rinse to Tube 2 to maximize lipid recovery.

Tube 2 — phase separation (12mL, 16×100mm)

  1. 7Add 0.75mL cold MS-grade water to Tube 2 to induce phase separation.
  2. 8Vortex for 1 minute.
  3. 9Centrifuge at 2000 rpm, 10 minutes, 4°C.
  4. 10Collect 1mL from the upper MTBE/lipid phase using a glass Pasteur pipet. Aspirate from the top, avoid disturbing the protein interface. Be consistent in pipet depth across all samples.
  5. 11Dry collected fraction under nitrogen stream or SpeedVac.
  6. 12Reconstitute in 1:1 chloroform:methanol at appropriate volume for downstream analysis.
⚠️
Blood samples: Heme and hemoglobin-derived porphyrins can co-extract into the upper phase. The cold workflow reduces this risk. If red/brown color persists in the upper phase, perform a back-wash with 0.5mL cold MS-grade water — vortex, re-centrifuge, re-collect upper phase.
📌
Solvent ratios: Final MTBE:MeOH:H₂O ratio is approximately 10:6:3 — within the validated Matyash range for full lipid class coverage.
ℹ️
All solvents chilled. Work on ice throughout. Centrifuge at 4°C. Lipid-rich phase collects at bottom. All chloroform handling in fume hood.

Tube 1 — extraction with punch (20mL, 16×150mm)

  1. 1Place 13mm glass fiber punch into a 20mL glass test tube (16×150mm).
  2. 2Add 2mL cold 2:1 chloroform:methanol. Ensure punch is fully submerged.
  3. 3Sonicate for 30 minutes in an ice bath. Monitor ice bath temperature — replenish ice if needed.
  4. 4Add 0.5mL cold water. Vortex for 5 minutes on ice.
  5. 5Transfer extract to a 12mL glass tube (Tube 2, 16×100mm), leaving punch behind.

Tube 2 — phase separation (12mL, 16×100mm)

  1. 6Add 0.5mL cold water and 0.5mL cold chloroform to Tube 2.
  2. 7Vortex for 1 minute.
  3. 8Centrifuge at 2000 rpm, 10 minutes, 4°C.
  4. 9Carefully remove and discard the upper aqueous phase without disturbing the interface.
  5. 10Collect 1mL from the lower chloroform/lipid phase using a glass Pasteur pipet. Insert pipet slowly through the interface layer. Be consistent in pipet depth across all samples.
  6. 11Dry collected fraction under nitrogen stream or SpeedVac.
  7. 12Reconstitute in 1:1 chloroform:methanol at appropriate volume for downstream analysis.
⚠️
Interface caution: The protein interface sits directly above the chloroform phase. When collecting the bottom layer, insert the Pasteur pipet slowly and avoid aspirating interface material. A consistent insertion depth across all samples is critical for reproducibility.
⚠️
Chloroform hazard: Carcinogen. All handling, transfers, and drying must be performed in a fume hood. Collect waste in dedicated halogenated waste containers.

Matrix-Specific Notes

🩸 Blood (1mL on 13mm punch)

  • • Confirm full saturation and even drying of punch before extraction
  • • Heme co-extraction risk reduced by cold workflow throughout
  • • If brown color persists in MTBE upper phase, back-wash with 0.5mL cold water
  • • High hemoglobin content — protein interface will be prominent
  • • Pre-soak in MeOH (MTBE method) especially beneficial for dried blood on glass fiber

🪴 Placenta (punch)

  • • Rich in long-chain PUFAs (DHA, AA) — keep cold throughout to minimize oxidation
  • • Higher tissue density than blood — 30 min sonication should be sufficient
  • • Monitor ice bath closely for placenta samples to protect unsaturated lipids
  • • May produce more emulsified interface than blood — increase spin time if needed
  • • Phospholipid content expected to be high — both methods should recover well
✅
General: Use the same glass Pasteur pipet insertion depth for every sample within a method. Document the volume collected per sample. Normalize to a fixed reconstitution volume rather than to collected volume.

Back-Calculation — Lipid Concentration

Assuming consistent solvent additions and negligible residual water from punches, total phase volumes are stable across samples. A fixed 1mL aliquot therefore represents the same fraction of the total extract for every sample, making concentrations directly comparable without a per-sample correction.

Parameter MTBE Method Bligh & Dyer
Estimated total phase volume ~3.2mL upper phase ~1.7mL lower phase
Volume collected 1mL 1mL
Fraction collected ~31% of extract ~59% of extract
Original sample volume 1mL blood or digest 1mL blood or digest
📐
Formula: Csample = Creconstituted × Vreconstitution / Vcollected fraction × Vtotal phase / Voriginal sample

Example (MTBE): Measured concentration in reconstituted extract = 50 µg/mL, reconstituted in 200µL, 1mL collected from ~3.2mL total phase, original sample = 1mL.
Csample = 50 × 0.2 / 1 × 3.2 / 1 = 32 µg/mL in original sample

Example (B&D): Same measured concentration = 50 µg/mL, reconstituted in 200µL, 1mL collected from ~1.7mL total phase, original sample = 1mL.
Csample = 50 × 0.2 / 1 × 1.7 / 1 = 17 µg/mL in original sample

Note: Total phase volume estimates are based on solvent additions alone. Verify against actual measured phase volumes in initial test runs and update these values accordingly.
