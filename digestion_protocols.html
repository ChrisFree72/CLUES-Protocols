Solvent & Reagent Preparation

⚠️ KOH Preparation Warning KOH dissolution in water is highly exothermic. Always add KOH pellets to water slowly — never add water to pellets. Mix slowly and allow to cool before use. Work in a fume hood with appropriate PPE.
⚠️ Hydrogen Peroxide Warning 30% hydrogen peroxide is a strong oxidizer. Handle with care, avoid skin contact, and work in a fume hood. Do not cap tubes tightly immediately after adding to samples — gas evolution can pressurize containers.

20% w/v KOH — 500mL

  1. Weigh 100g of KOH pellets and transfer to a 500mL glass vessel.
  2. Slowly add 500mL of filtered HPLC grade water.
  3. Cap with a glass stopper and mix slowly until the solution is transparent.
Used in: Method 1 (MAD)

5% w/v KOH — 500mL

  1. Weigh 25g of KOH pellets and transfer to a 500mL glass vessel.
  2. Slowly add 500mL of filtered HPLC grade water.
  3. Cap with a glass stopper and mix slowly until the solution is transparent.
Used in: Method 2 (Mild KOH) · pH adjustment

0.1% Tween-20 — 100mL

  1. Pipet 100µL of Tween-20 into a 100mL glass vessel.
  2. Slowly add 100mL of filtered HPLC grade water.
  3. Cap with a glass stopper and mix slowly until the solution is transparent.
Used in: Methods 1 & 2

Rinse Solution

  1. Add 100mL of filtered HPLC grade water to a 250mL glass vessel.
  2. Add 100mL of IPA to the same vessel.
  3. Cap and mix thoroughly.
Used in: All methods (filtration step)

8–10 pH Solution — 100mL

  1. Add 100mL of filtered HPLC grade water to a glass vessel.
  2. Add 5% KOH dropwise while monitoring pH continuously.
  3. Stop addition when pH is between 8 and 10.
Used in: Proteinase K · CaCl₂ · CREON preparation

400mM Tris-HCl (pH 8.0) — 250mL

  1. Add 100mL of 1M Tris-HCl to 150mL of filtered water and mix.
  2. Check pH and adjust to 8.0 if needed.
Used in: Methods 3 & 4 (Enzymatic)

1mg/mL Proteinase K

  1. Weigh 1–10mg of proteinase K.
  2. Add an equal volume in mL of 8–10 pH solution (e.g., 5mg → 5mL).
  3. Mix gently until fully dissolved.
Used in: Methods 3 & 4 (Enzymatic)

5mM CaCl₂ — 100mL

  1. Weigh 55.5mg of CaCl₂.
  2. Transfer to a glass vessel containing 100mL of 8–10 pH solution.
  3. Mix until fully dissolved.
Used in: Methods 3 & 4 (Enzymatic)

2.5% CREON Enzyme Solution — 10mL

  1. Transfer 240mg of CREON pellets to a glass vessel containing 10mL of 8–10 pH solution.
  2. Allow to sit for approximately 1 hour.
  3. Sonicate lightly until the pellets are fully dissolved.
Used in: Methods 3 & 4 (Enzymatic)

Digestion Methods

📌 Strong KOH Microwave Assisted Digestion (MAD) Best suited for tissue samples. Uses high-concentration KOH with microwave-assisted heating for rapid, aggressive digestion. An overnight incubation follows the microwave step before filtration.

Phase 1 — Sample Setup Bench

  1. Weigh 1g of tissue sample and transfer to a 20mL test tube.
  2. Add 5mL of 20% KOH using a glass pipet.
  3. Add 1mL of 0.1% Tween-20.
  4. Add 1mL of pure ethanol.
  5. Vortex thoroughly to ensure the digestion mixture is well combined.

Phase 2 — Microwave Digestion Microwave

  1. Place each 20mL tube into a microwave vessel and cap securely.
  2. Load the microwave vessel compartment into the microwave.
  3. Run the 35-minute microwave digestion protocol.
  4. Allow samples to sit overnight before proceeding to filtration.

Phase 3 — Post-Digestion Bench

  1. Remove tubes from the microwave vessel compartment and individual vessels.
  2. Transfer samples to the prep room for filtration.
➡️ Proceed to Filtration — 47mm filter / 15-minute rinse protocol See the Filtration section. MAD samples use 47mm GF/F filters with a 15-minute rinse hold time.
📌 Mild KOH Digestion Suitable for both biological fluids (1mL) and tissue samples (1g). Uses lower-concentration KOH with a shake incubator at 30°C over 24 hours. A gentler approach than MAD, compatible with a wider range of sample types.

Phase 1 — Sample Setup Bench

  1. Transfer 1mL of biological fluid or weigh 1g of tissue into a 20mL test tube.
  2. Add 5mL of 5% KOH using a glass pipet.
  3. Add 1mL of 0.1% Tween-20.
  4. Add 1mL of pure ethanol.
  5. Vortex thoroughly to ensure the digestion mixture is well combined.

Phase 2 — Shake Incubation Incubator

  1. Place the 20mL tubes into the shake incubator.
  2. Incubate at 30°C, 180 rpm for 24 hours.
  3. Remove tubes from the shake incubator upon completion.
  4. Transfer samples to the prep room for filtration.
➡️ Proceed to Filtration — 47mm or 25mm filter protocol See the Filtration section. Rinse hold time is 30 seconds for biological fluids and 15 minutes for tissue samples.
📌 Enzymatic Digestion with Peroxide Cleanup (Rauert 2025) Suitable for both biological fluids (1mL) and tissue samples (1g). Multi-step enzymatic digestion followed by hydrogen peroxide oxidative cleanup. Longest protocol — approximately 52+ hours total.
⚠️ Hydrogen Peroxide Step 30% H₂O₂ is added during this protocol. Work in a fume hood at all times during this step. Do not seal tubes tightly immediately after addition and monitor for excessive gas evolution.

Phase 1 — Initial Incubation Incubator

  1. Transfer 1mL of biological fluid or weigh 1g of tissue into a 20mL test tube.
  2. Add 10mL of 400mM Tris-HCl solution.
  3. Incubate at 60°C in the shake incubator for 1 hour.
  4. Remove tubes and allow the solution to cool to room temperature before proceeding.

Phase 2 — Proteinase K Digestion Incubator

  1. Add 1mL of 5mM CaCl₂ solution to each tube.
  2. Add 100µL of 1mg/mL Proteinase K solution to each tube.
  3. Shake incubate at 37°C for 2 hours.

Phase 3 — CREON Enzyme Digestion Incubator

  1. Adjust the pH of each sample to 10 using 5% KOH added dropwise.
  2. Add 2mL of 2.5% CREON enzyme solution to each tube.
  3. Incubate at 37°C for 3 hours.

Phase 4 — Hydrogen Peroxide Oxidative Cleanup Fume Hood

  1. In the fume hood, add 8mL of 30% hydrogen peroxide to each tube.
  2. Leave tubes loosely capped and digest at room temperature, 180 rpm for 48 hours.
  3. Heat samples to 60°C prior to filtration.
  4. Remove tubes from the shake incubator and transfer to the prep room for filtration.
➡️ Proceed to Filtration — 47mm or 25mm filter protocol Rinse hold time is 30 seconds for biological fluids and 15 minutes for tissue samples.
📌 Enzymatic Digestion — In-House (No Peroxide) Suitable for both biological fluids (1mL) and tissue samples (1g). Simplified enzymatic digestion without the hydrogen peroxide oxidative cleanup step. Approximately 51+ hours total.

Phase 1 — Initial Incubation Incubator

  1. Transfer 1mL of biological fluid or weigh 1g of tissue into a 20mL test tube.
  2. Add 10mL of 400mM Tris-HCl solution.
  3. Incubate at 60°C in the shake incubator for 1 hour.
  4. Remove tubes and allow the solution to cool to room temperature before proceeding.

Phase 2 — Proteinase K + CREON Digestion Incubator

  1. Add 1mL of 5mM CaCl₂ solution to each tube.
  2. Add 100µL of 1mg/mL Proteinase K solution to each tube.
  3. Add 2mL of 2.5% CREON enzyme solution to each tube.
  4. Digest at 37°C, 180 rpm for 48 hours.
  5. Heat samples to 60°C prior to filtration.
  6. Remove tubes from the shake incubator and transfer to the prep room for filtration.
➡️ Proceed to Filtration — 47mm or 25mm filter protocol Rinse hold time is 30 seconds for biological fluids and 15 minutes for tissue samples.

Filtration & Filter Processing

📌 Filter Size Selection Method 1 (MAD) uses 47mm GF/F filters only. Methods 2, 3, and 4 may use either 47mm (Millipore glass holder) or 25mm (Adventec glass holder) filters depending on sample volume. Rinse hold time differs by sample type — see step 5 below.

Filter Setup

  1. Load the appropriate GF/F filters onto the glass filter holders — 47mm filters onto Millipore 47mm holders, or 25mm filters onto Adventec 25mm holders.
  2. Load the filter holders onto the vacuum manifold in the fume hood.
  3. Using the Teflon squeeze bottle, soak each filter with rinse solution (water:IPA).
  4. Turn on the vacuum and pull the rinse solution through the filter to pre-wet and condition it.

Sample Filtration

  1. Pour the digested sample from the test tube directly into the glass filter holder.
  2. Turn on the vacuum to pull the digest through the filter.
  3. Add approximately 5mL of rinse solution to the empty test tube and rinse the tube walls thoroughly.
  4. Pour the tube rinse into the glass filter holder.
  5. Allow the rinse solution to sit on the filter for the appropriate hold time:
    • Biological fluids: 30 seconds
    • Tissue samples: 15 minutes
    This hold time allows residual lipids and water-soluble proteins to be removed from the filter surface.
  6. Turn on the vacuum and pull the rinse solution through until the filter is completely dry.

Filter Punching & Storage

  1. Remove the dried filter from the glass filter holder.
  2. Punch out the appropriate number of sample discs:
    • 47mm filter: Punch 4 × 13mm discs
    • 25mm filter: Punch 1 × 15mm disc
  3. Fold 1 × 13mm punch (or the 1 × 15mm punch for 25mm filters) into a Frontier pyrolysis cup for analysis.
  4. Store the remaining 3 × 13mm punches in a labeled 20mL scintillation vial for archiving.
